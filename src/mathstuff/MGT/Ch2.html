2.1.  Preliminaries on Fields

Let F/E be a field extension. We denote by mE⁢(α,X) the minimal polynomial of α over E.

Definition 2.1.1.

Let F/E be a field extension. α∈F is called separable over E if mE⁢(α) has distinct roots in an algebraic closure of E. The extension F/E is said to be separable if for every α∈E, α is separable.

Lemma 2.1.2.

Let F be a finite field with characteristic p. Then F/𝔽p is a separable extension.

Proof.

If the cardinality of F=pe, then for any α∈F, we have that m𝔽p⁢(α,X)|Xpe−X which has all distinct roots. ∎

Definition 2.1.3.

Let F be a field. We define AutE⁢(F):={f∈Aut⁢(F):f|E=i⁢d}.

Remark 1.

If Q is the prime subfield of F, then AutQ⁢(F)=Aut⁢(F).

Theorem 2.1.4.

If F/E is a field extension, and f,g∈E⁢[x], then the gcd of f,g in E is equal to the gcd of f,g in F. If f has a double root, then gcd⁡(f,f′)≠1.

Corollary 2.1.5.

If F/E is an extension with char⁢(E)=0, then F/E is a separable extension.
