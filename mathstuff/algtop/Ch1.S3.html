3.  Covering Spaces

Definition 3.1.

Let p:E→Bp:E\rightarrow B be a surjective map of topological spaces. The open set UU of BB is said to be evenly covered by BB if p−1⁢(U)p^{-1}(U) can be written as the disjoint union of open sets VαV_{\alpha} such that the restriction of pp to VαV_{\alpha} is a homeomorphism onto UU. The collection {Vα}\{V_{\alpha}\} will be called a partition of p−1⁢(U)p^{-1}(U) into slices

Definition 3.2 (Covering space).

Let p:E→Bp:E\rightarrow B be a surjective map of topological sapces. If for every b∈Bb\in B, there is an open U⊃{b}U\supset\{b\} such that UU is evenly covered by pp, then EE is said to be a covering space for BB and pp is said to be a covering map .

That is to say, if BB is locally evenly covered by pp, then EE is a covering space for BB.

Theorem 3.5.

Proof.

Since pp is a covering map, there exists an open cover {Uα}α∈J\{U_{\alpha}\}_{\alpha\in J} of BB such that each UαU_{\alpha} is evenly covered by pp. Now, note that {f−1⁢(Uα)}α∈J\{f^{-1}(U_{\alpha})\}_{\alpha\in J} is an open cover of [0,1][0,1], and by the lebesgue number lemma, there is a δ>0\delta>0 with the property that for any x∈[0,1]x\in[0,1], we have (x,x+δ)⊆f−1⁢(Uα)(x,x+\delta)\subseteq f^{-1}(U_{\alpha}) for some α∈J\alpha\in J. Theorem 3.8.

We have the isomorphism

π1⁢(S1)≅ℤ\pi_{1}(S^{1})\cong\mathbb{Z}
Proof.

The map t↦(cos⁡(2⁢π⁢t),sin⁡(2⁢π⁢t))t\mapsto(\cos(2\pi t),\sin(2\pi t)) is a covering map, and we take the lifting correspondence ϕ:π1⁢(S1)≅π1⁢(S1,(0,1))→p−1⁢((0,1))=ℤ\phi:\pi_{1}(S^{1})\cong\pi_{1}(S^{1},(0,1))\rightarrow p^{-1}((0,1))=\mathbb{Z} w.r.t 0. This map is certainly bijective, since ℝ\mathbb{R} is simply connected. We need to show that this map is a homomorphism. To that end, let ϕ⁢([f])=m,ϕ⁢([g])=n\phi([f])=m,\phi([g])=n. Note that if f~,g~\tilde{f},\tilde{g} are the unique lifts of f,gf,g along pp, then f~⋆(m+g~)\tilde{f}\star(m+\tilde{g}) goes from 0 to m+nm+n, and a simple computation shows that it is a lifting of f⋆gf\star g along pp. Thus ϕ⁢([f]⋆[g])=m+n=ϕ⁢([f])+ϕ⁢([g])\phi([f]\star[g])=m+n=\phi([f])+\phi([g]). ∎
