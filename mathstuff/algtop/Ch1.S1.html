1.  Homotopy

Definition 1.1 (Homotopic maps).

Let f,f′:X→Yf,f^{\prime}:X\rightarrow Y be two maps. We say that ff is homotopic to f′f^{\prime} if there exists a continuous map F:X×I→YF:X\times I\rightarrow Y such that:

∀x∈X:F⁢(x,0)=f⁢(x)⁢ and ⁢F⁢(x,1)=f′⁢(x)\forall x\in X:F(x,0)=f(x)\text{ and }F(x,1)=f^{\prime}(x)

and the map ff is said to be homotopic to ff, written f≃f′f\simeq f^{\prime}.

Definition 1.2 (Path Homotopy).

Two paths f,g:[0,1]→Xf,g:[0,1]\rightarrow X with same start and end points,say a,ba,b respectively are said to be path homotopic if there exists a homotopy F:I×I→XF:I\times I\rightarrow X such that

∀t∈[0,1]:F⁢(0,t)=a,F⁢(1,t)=b\forall t\in[0,1]:F(0,t)=a,F(1,t)=b

and we write ≃p\simeq_{p}

Ex 1.1.

The relations ≃\simeq and ≃p\simeq_{p} are equivalence relations
