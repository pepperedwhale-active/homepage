6.  Homology Groups of Spheres

Theorem 6.1 (Homology Groups of Sphere).

For n≥1n\geq 1, 𝕊n\mathbb{S}^{n} has the following singular homology groups:

Proof.

We use the Mayer-Vietoris sequence as follows. Let N,SN,S denote the north and south poles , and let U=𝕊n⁢{N},V=𝕊n⁢{S}U=\mathbb{S}^{n}\ \{N\},V=\mathbb{S}^{n}\{S\}. Mayer-Vietoris gives us:

Hp⁢(U)⊕Hp⁢(V)→Hp⁢(𝕊n)⁢→∂∗⁢Hp−1⁢(U∩V)→Hp−1⁢(U)⊕Hp−1⁢(V).H_{p}(U)\oplus H_{p}(V)\rightarrow H_{p}(\mathbb{S}^{n})\overset{\partial_{% \ast}}{\rightarrow}H_{p-1}(U\cap V)\rightarrow H_{p-1}(U)\oplus H_{p-1}(V).

Since U,VU,V are contractible, when p>1p>1, we have that the following sequence is exact:

0→Hp⁢(𝕊n)⁢→∂∗⁢Hp−1⁢(U∩V)→0.0\rightarrow H_{p}(\mathbb{S}^{n})\overset{\partial_{\ast}}{\rightarrow}H_{p-1% }(U\cap V)\rightarrow 0.

But U∩VU\cap V is homotopy equivalent to 𝕊n−1\mathbb{S}^{n-1}, so we have Hp⁢(𝕊n)≅Hp−1⁢(𝕊n−1)H_{p}(\mathbb{S}^{n})\cong H_{p-1}(\mathbb{S}^{n-1}). By induction, we are done. ∎
